Online Game Addiction

Game addiction, also known as Gaming disorder, can negatively affect a student’s academic performance. Students may lose focus on their studies, skip assignments, and get lower grades because of excessive gaming.

Symptoms

  • Playing video games too much, even for many hours every day
  • Playing games all the time and not stopping even when needed
  • Choosing games over schoolwork, sleep, and other important activities
  • Feeling upset or irritated when not allowed to play
  • Losing focus on studies because of constant gaming

Effects

  • Poor academic performance and low grades
  • Lack of sleep and tiredness during school
  • Less focus and poor concentration in class
  • Reduced social interaction with family and friends
  • Stress, anxiety, or mood changes

Prevention

  • Set a time limit for playing video games each day
  • Prioritize schoolwork before playing games
  • Take breaks and do other activities like sports or hobbies
  • Ask help from parents or teachers to manage gaming time
  • Follow a proper sleep schedule

Help & Contact

If you are struggling with game addiction (Gaming disorder), you can contact us for help and support in managing your time and improving your studies.
